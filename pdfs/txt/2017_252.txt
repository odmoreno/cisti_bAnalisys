Alternative communication and use of assistive devices in intensive care unit: Case study
ABSTRACT
The communication is fundamental in the relationship between people. It is affected by speech, writing or gestures. In certain situations the oral communication may be impaired, writing disabled and gestures not being interpreted in a precise way. Such situations are common among patients hospitalized in an intensive care setting. This study aimed to verify the use of an assistive device as an alternative method of communication between the health team and the patients hospitalized in an intensive care unit. The research is a case study of qualitative approach. The method used to analyze the data collected was the Bardin content analysis. The guiding categories of the study were: Communication and social interaction; Alternative technologies; Life after leaving the hospital. The communication provided by the mobile assistive device has benefited the construction of basic and complex messages between the patient, family members and health professionals.
